Pressed & Poured


This integrated campaign merges Rolling Stone’s music legacy with Patrón’s premium identity, positioning both brands as makers of timeless “hits.” The idea comes to life across multiple mediums, including a magazine ad and a bus shelter execution featuring an overhead composition of a vinyl record with a Patrón bottle burned into its surface, alongside tactile details like a lime-garnished glass and scattered records. A second magazine ad expands the concept with a wall of silver, gold, and platinum records, each branded with the Patrón bottle, reinforcing the campaign line: “Some hits are pressed, others are poured.”


The campaign extends into digital through a custom-designed website that mirrors the same elevated aesthetic. Featuring curated product photography and a seamless, swipeable interface, the site emphasizes both usability and visual storytelling. Together, these executions create a cohesive system that bridges music and mixology, elevating Patrón into a cultural symbol of taste and status.


Timeless Heinz


Created to celebrate 150 years of Heinz, this campaign centers on the idea of timelessness, using the visual language of time to reinforce the brand’s enduring legacy. Expressed through a series of bus shelter advertisements, each execution transforms everyday Heinz products into symbolic representations of time. A white plate becomes a clock, with numbers written in ketchup and mustard hands marking the hours, paired with the line “Timeless Flavor.”


Additional executions continue this visual metaphor, including an hourglass formed from mustard with ketchup flowing as sand, and an infinity symbol drawn in mustard to represent continuity. Paired with the lines “Timeless Taste” and “Timeless Legacy,” these minimalist yet striking visuals position Heinz as a brand that has not only stood the test of time, but remains culturally and visually relevant.


Recent Future Studios


This body of work showcases a series of promotional designs created for the independent film studio Recent Future Studios. The project includes official movie posters for The Sunset Stranger and Soul of the Dark, along with a range of supporting promotional fliers distributed across Temple University’s campus. Each piece was designed to generate intrigue and build a distinct visual identity for the films while maintaining consistency across formats.


The designs lean heavily into bold, saturated color palettes and dynamic compositions, using structured grid systems to organize layered photo stills from each film. This approach creates a balance between chaos and control, reflecting the tone of the films while ensuring clarity in communication. Together, the posters and fliers form a cohesive campaign that captures attention in crowded environments and reinforces the studio’s emerging creative voice.


1V1 


1V1 is a campaign centered on the competitive spirit of gaming, visualizing the dynamic between Player 1 and Player 2. Executed through two magazine ads and a billboard, the campaign uses a bold palette of reds, blues, and beige to distinguish opposing sides while maintaining a cohesive look. The first magazine ad features a retro-inspired living room split down the middle, contrasting two nearly identical spaces with subtle differences, anchored by the line “The Game is Never Over.”


The second magazine ad shifts to a stylized office scene, where two silhouetted figures face off against a surreal city backdrop. The billboard simplifies these ideas into a bold composition of two back-to-back players in red and blue. Across all executions, the campaign blends retro nostalgia with clean, modern design to highlight SEGA’s legacy of competitive play.


FUNERAL V.


Created in collaboration with Alexandra Pompan and Margo Maakestad, FUNERAL V. is an experiential campaign designed to generate social buzz through mystery and participation. Centered around a secret, Met Gala–style event, the concept invites consumers to uncover clues and earn access to an exclusive “funeral” for competing service providers. The campaign lives primarily on Instagram, where a series of three grid posts form a cohesive visual when viewed on the profile page, using a bold red, black, and white palette to establish a dark, cinematic tone.


The campaign extends into the physical world through street-level fliers, reinforcing the sense of intrigue and discovery. Featuring striking imagery of stacked skulls and lunar phases, the designs pair minimal yet provocative copy like “Witness the end” with a website link and password for entry. Blending digital storytelling with real-world interaction, the campaign positions Verizon as dominant and culturally aware, turning a service provider into an experience.


Girard College


During my time as a social media intern at the Girard College Founder’s Hall Museum, I developed a range of visual content aimed at increasing engagement and highlighting the institution’s history. My work included designing social media posts, large-scale banners for the EMS building, and contributing to recurring content series such as “Did You Know?” and “Look Familiar?” These pieces were created to make archival information more accessible and visually engaging for a broader audience.


A key part of the role involved working with the museum’s archival photography, incorporating striking historical images into modern, cohesive designs. I created multiple layout systems that combined these visuals with clean typography and structured compositions, ensuring consistency across platforms. The result was a body of work that both honored the history of Girard College and reintroduced it through a contemporary design lens.
